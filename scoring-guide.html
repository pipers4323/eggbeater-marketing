What Is Box Scoring?

A real-time game record, built in your browser

Box scoring means recording every significant event in a game — goals, exclusions, timeouts, saves — as it happens, right from your phone or tablet. Eggbeater turns that live data into a running score, a detailed play-by-play log, and player stat summaries that parents can follow from anywhere.

No special equipment needed. No app to install. Just open the Eggbeater app, unlock Scorer Mode, and you're ready to go.

📡

Real-time to parents

Every goal and quarter change pushes to parent devices within seconds — no page refresh required.

📊

Full play-by-play

Goals, exclusions, saves, and timeouts are time-stamped and attributed to individual players.

🏅

Player stats

Goals, assists, and GK Saves accumulate on each player's card across the whole tournament.

🔔

Instant alerts

GroupMe and Telegram notifications fire automatically when games start, goals are scored, and final scores are posted.

Scorer Login

🔑

Scorer Mode is password-protected so only authorized people can enter live scores. Your club admin sets the password — ask your head coach or tournament director if you don't have it.

Open the Eggbeater app and go to the Scores tab

Tap the Scores tab at the bottom of the screen. You'll see a list of today's games with a 🔒 Scorer Login button in the top-right corner of the tab.

Tap "Scorer Login" and enter the password

A password prompt will appear. Type the scorer password your admin gave you and tap Unlock. The lock icon will turn into a green ✅ confirming you're in Scorer Mode.

You're in — Scorer Mode stays active

Once unlocked, Scorer Mode stays on for your session — even if you switch tabs or briefly leave the app. You'll see a green Scorer Mode Active bar at the top of the Scores tab. Tap 🔒 Lock to log out when you're done for the day.

⚠️

Don't share the scorer password publicly. Anyone with it can change live scores. If the password is compromised, your admin can update it from the Admin panel and re-share it with authorized scorers only.

The Scores Tab

What you see in Scorer Mode

Once logged in, the Scores tab shows a card for every upcoming and in-progress game. Games are grouped by date and sorted by scheduled start time. Each card shows:

  • Game number — e.g., Game 14
  • Teams — your team vs. opponent, with cap colors
  • Scheduled time and location
  • Current score and period — once the game is started
  • A Score button — tap to open the full scoring panel for that game

Game status indicators

Pre-Game Game hasn't started yet. Clock shows the full quarter length (e.g., 7:00 or 8:00).
🔴 LIVE Game is currently being scored. Score and clock are updating in real-time for all viewers.
Final Game is complete. The card moves to the History tab once finalized.

Opening a Game

Find your game on the Scores tab

Locate the game card for the match you're about to score. Games are sorted by start time so your next game is always near the top.

Tap the Score button

Tap the blue Score button on the game card. The full scoring panel opens, filling most of your screen.

Confirm the teams before the whistle

You'll see the two teams, a game clock, and all the event buttons. Take a moment to confirm the correct game is open — check the game number and opponent name at the top of the panel. Then you're ready to go.

💡

You can score only one game at a time. If you need to switch games mid-tournament, close the current panel and open the correct game from the Scores tab.

5

The Clock

How the game clock works

Eggbeater has a built-in countdown clock for each quarter. It runs automatically once you tap Start and ticks down on its own — you don't need to babysit it. The clock is used to time-stamp events in the play-by-play log; it runs independently of the physical scoreboard at the pool.

The clock display sits near the top of the scoring panel, showing minutes and seconds remaining in the current period (e.g., 7:00 at the start of Q1).

Clock buttons explained

▶ Start Begins the automatic countdown for the current quarter. Tap this when the referee blows the whistle to start play. The clock ticks down on its own — you don't need to touch it again until the quarter ends or there's a significant stoppage.
⏸ Stop Pauses the countdown. Use this for extended stoppages — an injury timeout, equipment issue, or to re-sync with the pool clock. Tap ▶ Start again to resume.
↩ Pre Returns the game to Pre-Game (not-started) state. This is an undo for accidentally starting a game too early. All events and the score are cleared, and the clock resets to the full quarter length. Only use Pre before meaningful play has begun.
⟳ Reset Resets the clock to the full quarter length without changing the period or score. Useful if the physical clock was reset at the pool and you need to re-sync mid-quarter.
⚠️

Pre clears all events and the score. If Q1 is already underway with goals recorded, using Pre will wipe everything — the game reverts to 0–0 and not-started. To simply pause play mid-quarter without losing data, use Stop instead.

Keeping the clock roughly in sync

Water polo clocks stop frequently — on goals, exclusions, timeouts, and ball out of bounds. You don't need to stop and start the Eggbeater clock for every single whistle. Most scorers let the Eggbeater clock run continuously through normal stoppages. The time-stamps on events are reference points, not official timing.

If you fall significantly behind or ahead of the pool clock, tap Stop, adjust if needed, then tap ▶ Start when play resumes.

Recording Goals

⚽

Goals are the most important events to record accurately. Every goal immediately updates the live score seen by parents and triggers the Telegram/GroupMe goal notification.

Tap the goal button for the scoring team

There are two large + Goal buttons in the scoring panel — one for your team and one for the opponent. Tap the button for whichever team just scored. The current clock time is captured automatically.

Enter the scorer's cap number

A number pad appears. Type the cap number of the player who scored (e.g., 7 for cap 7). If you don't know the number in the moment, you can leave it blank — the goal will still count, just without player attribution. Tap Save to confirm.

Select the goal type

Choose from the options shown:

  • Regular — a standard field goal during even-strength play
  • 6-on-5 (Power Play) — scored while the opposing team is playing shorthanded due to an exclusion
  • Penalty Shot — awarded by the referee for a major foul

Score updates instantly across all devices

The scoreboard at the top of the panel increments immediately. Parents following remotely will see the updated score within a few seconds. The goal also appears in the play-by-play log below the score.

Correcting a goal entered by mistake

If you tap the wrong team's goal button, find the incorrect entry in the play-by-play log and tap the Delete (🗑 trash) icon next to it. The score decreases by one and the event is removed. Then record the correct goal.

Shot attempts and misses

Tap Miss to log a shot that didn't go in. This records a shot attempt for the shooter and a save opportunity for the goalkeeper — no score change. Shot tracking is optional but gives coaches useful data post-game.

7

Exclusions & Brutality Cards

Exclusions (20-second ejections)

An exclusion is a 20-second ejection for a major foul — the offending player must leave the water and their team plays shorthanded. To record an exclusion:

  1. Tap Exclusion for the team whose player was excluded.
  2. Enter the excluded player's cap number.
  3. Tap Save. The event logs with the current clock time and the player's personal exclusion count increments.

A player who accumulates three exclusions in a game is disqualified for the remainder of the match. Eggbeater tracks this automatically and shows a warning on the third exclusion.

Brutality cards

A brutality card is the most severe penalty in water polo — the offending player is excluded for the rest of the game and their team plays a man down for four minutes. To record one:

  1. Tap Brutality for the offending team.
  2. Enter the player's cap number and confirm.

Brutality cards are logged separately from regular exclusions in the play-by-play and appear prominently in the post-game box score summary.

💡

Recording an exclusion does not automatically start a 6-on-5 power-play clock in the app — just log the exclusion event and continue. When the excluded team scores (ending the 6-on-5), record it as a regular goal. You'll select the 6-on-5 goal type when the other team converts during the power play.

8

Assists & GK Saves

Assists

An assist is credited to the player whose pass directly led to a goal. Assists are optional — record them when you're confident about who made the key pass and it doesn't slow you down during play.

To add an assist: immediately after recording a goal, enter the assisting player's cap number in the assist field that appears in the goal prompt. Or, find the goal in the play-by-play log and tap Add Assist to add it after the fact.

GK Saves

A GK Save is credited to the goalkeeper who stopped a shot. Tap GK Save and enter the goalkeeper's cap number. GK Saves accumulate in the goalkeeper's individual stats and show up in the post-game box score.

💡

Goals and exclusions first. Assists and GK Saves add depth to the stats, but if the game is moving fast, skip them and focus on score-affecting events. You can always backfill optional stats between quarters.

9

Timeouts

Recording a timeout

Each team typically gets one timeout per game (exact rules vary by age group and tournament format). When a team calls a timeout:

  1. Tap Timeout for the team that called it.
  2. The timeout is logged with the current clock time and the team's timeout usage is recorded.
  3. Tap ⏸ Stop on the clock to pause the countdown while the teams huddle. Tap ▶ Start when the referee restarts play.
10

Between Quarters

The game period flow

A standard water polo game moves through these periods in order:

Pre-Game → Q1 → Q2 → Half → Q3 → Q4 → Final / OT / Shootout

You advance manually by tapping the End Quarter button when a period is over. The app will ask you to confirm before advancing to the next period.

Quarter break checklist

  • Wait for the referee's final whistle ending the quarter.
  • If the app clock is still running, tap ⏸ Stop.
  • Tap End Q1 (or whichever quarter just ended). The app moves to the break state and the clock resets.
  • When teams are back in the water and the referee is ready to begin, tap ▶ Start to begin the next quarter's countdown.

Halftime

After Q2, tapping End Q2 puts the game into Half state. The clock resets to the full Q3 length. Halftime is typically a few minutes — don't tap ▶ Start until both teams are back in the water and the referee signals to begin Q3.

11

Overtime

When the score is tied after Q4

If the game is tied at the end of Q4, tap End Q4 and the app will offer you three options:

Overtime Proceed to an OT period. The clock resets to the OT length (often 3:00 or 5:00 sudden death, depending on tournament rules). Goals and events are recorded exactly the same as in regular periods. Tap ▶ Start when OT begins.
Shootout Skip OT and go straight to a penalty shootout. See Section 12 below for full shootout instructions.
Final (tie) Record the game as a draw — some pool-play formats don't play overtime.
💡

If OT also ends tied, tap End OT and choose Shootout to proceed to penalty shots, or play additional OT periods if tournament rules require it.

12

Shootout Mode

How a shootout works

A shootout is a penalty shot competition. Teams alternate taking shots — typically 5 shots per team, one at a time. The team that converts more shots wins. If still tied after 5 rounds, the shootout continues in sudden death: one shot per team per round until one team leads after a completed round.

Enter Shootout Mode

When the referee announces a shootout, tap End Q4 (or End OT) → Shootout. The scoring panel switches to a simplified shootout view showing both teams' attempt counters and a running shootout tally. Parents automatically receive a Shootout Alert notification at this moment.

Record each shot attempt

For each shot, tap one of two buttons for the shooting team:

  • Goal ✓ — the shot went in. That team's shootout count increases by 1.
  • Miss ✗ — the shot was saved, hit the post, or missed. No score change.

Enter the shooting player's cap number when prompted, then alternate — your team shoots, then the opponent, repeat until the shootout is decided.

Finalize when complete

After 5 rounds (or when the winner is mathematically decided), a Finalize button appears. Tap it to end the game. The winning team is shown and the final score notification fires to all parent devices.

💡

During a shootout the main game clock is not used. Don't worry about starting or stopping it — just focus on logging each attempt accurately.

13

Finalizing the Game

Advance past the last period

When the final whistle blows, stop the clock and tap the End Q4 (or End OT / End Shootout) button. Select Final when prompted. The game status changes to Final in the scoring panel.

Review the box score

Take a moment to look over the play-by-play and confirm the score looks right. If you spot a missed goal or an incorrect event, you can still delete or add entries at this stage. Once you're satisfied, proceed.

Tap "Finalize Game"

Tap the Finalize Game button. The score is locked, the game moves off the Scores tab into the History tab, and a final-score notification fires to all parent devices. The game is complete — great work!

⚠️

Once finalized, the game is marked final in the system. If a correction is needed afterward, ask your club admin — they can reopen the game from the Admin panel.

14

Live Broadcast & Alerts

What parents see in real-time

Every time you record a goal, change the period, or finalize a game, Eggbeater broadcasts the update to all parent devices following that game — no page refresh needed. Parents see:

  • The current score and period, updating live
  • A 🔴 LIVE badge on the game card while scoring is active
  • The play-by-play log building in real-time when they tap into the game detail

Automatic notifications

If your club has a Telegram or GroupMe channel connected, the following alerts fire automatically without you doing anything extra:

Game started Fires when you tap ▶ Start at the beginning of Q1, alerting families the game is underway.
Goal scored Fires immediately when a goal is confirmed, with the scorer's name (if entered) and the updated score.
Shootout alert Fires when Shootout Mode is entered, so families know it's down to penalty shots.
Final score Fires when the game is finalized with the full box score summary.
🎉

Parents who can't be at the pool can follow every goal in near real-time. The more complete your scoring, the richer the experience — even grandparents in another state can celebrate goals the moment they happen.
